# Category: Unacceptable Risk (Prohibited AI Practices)

## 1. Definition

AI systems falling into this category are considered to pose a clear threat to the safety, livelihoods, and fundamental rights of people. **Their use is prohibited within the European Union under the AI Act.**

These systems are deemed incompatible with EU values and fundamental rights due to the unacceptable level of risk they introduce.

## 2. Examples of Prohibited AI Practices

The AI Act explicitly bans the following types of AI systems:

- **Cognitive Behavioral Manipulation:** AI systems that deploy subliminal techniques beyond a person’s consciousness or exploit vulnerabilities of specific groups (due to age, physical or mental disability) to materially distort their behavior in a way likely to cause physical or psychological harm.
- **Social Scoring by Public Authorities:** AI systems used by public authorities for the purpose of social scoring, leading to detrimental or unfavorable treatment in social contexts unrelated to the original data collection or that is unjustified or disproportionate.
- **Real-time Remote Biometric Identification in Publicly Accessible Spaces for Law Enforcement:** The use of 'real-time' remote biometric identification systems (e.g., facial recognition) in publicly accessible spaces for law enforcement purposes is generally prohibited, with very narrow, strictly defined exceptions (e.g., searching for specific victims of crime, preventing imminent threats like terrorism, locating suspects of serious crimes). These exceptions require prior judicial or administrative authorization and are subject to strict limitations.
- **Emotion Recognition in Workplaces and Educational Institutions:** AI systems designed to infer emotions or intentions of individuals in the context of workplaces and educational institutions, except for medical or safety reasons.
- **Predictive Policing Based Solely on Profiling:** AI systems used for predictive policing that rely solely on profiling an individual or assessing personality traits and characteristics to predict criminal behavior.
- **Indiscriminate Scraping for Facial Recognition Databases:** AI systems that create or expand facial recognition databases through the untargeted scraping of facial images from the internet or CCTV footage.

## 3. Key Considerations

- **Absolute Prohibition:** Unlike other categories, there are no compliance pathways for these systems; they are simply banned.
- **Focus on Harm:** The prohibitions target AI applications with a high likelihood of causing significant harm or infringing on fundamental rights in unacceptable ways.
- **Narrow Exceptions:** For real-time biometric identification by law enforcement, the exceptions are extremely limited and heavily regulated.

## 4. Associated Technologies & Risks

While the prohibition focuses on the _application_, certain technologies are often associated with these banned practices:

- **Biometric Identification Systems (Real-time, Remote):** Facial recognition, gait analysis, etc., when used by law enforcement in public spaces without meeting strict exception criteria.
  - _Risk:_ Mass surveillance, chilling effects on freedom of assembly, potential for discrimination and errors.
- **Emotion Recognition Technology:** Systems analyzing facial expressions, voice patterns, or physiological signals to infer emotional states.
  - _Risk:_ Inaccuracy, potential for discrimination, invasion of privacy, manipulation in specific contexts (workplace, education).
- **Profiling Algorithms:** Systems analyzing data to predict behavior or characteristics.
  - _Risk:_ Reinforcement of biases, discriminatory outcomes, lack of transparency, potential for misuse in predictive policing or social scoring.
- **Natural Language Processing (NLP) & Generative AI:** Can be misused to create manipulative content or deepfakes targeting vulnerable groups.
  - _Risk:_ Disinformation, manipulation, psychological harm.

## 5. Implications for Projects

If a proposed AI project involves any of the applications listed in Section 2, it is **prohibited** under the EU AI Act and should not be pursued for deployment within the EU. Development teams must ensure their AI systems do not fall into these categories or enable such prohibited uses.
