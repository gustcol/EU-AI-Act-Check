# Glossary of Key Terms (EU AI Act - Based on Appendix B)

This glossary defines key terms as used in the context of the EU AI Act, based on the provided reference document.

---

**Artificial Intelligence System (AI System)**
: Software developed with one or more techniques listed in Annex I of the AI Act that can, for a given set of human-defined objectives, generate outputs such as content, predictions, recommendations, or decisions influencing physical or virtual environments.

**CE Marking**
: A manufacturer's declaration that products comply with EU health, safety, and environmental protection requirements. Required for many high-risk AI systems before market placement.

**Conformity Assessment**
: The process demonstrating whether the requirements relating to an AI system (particularly high-risk systems) under the AI Act have been fulfilled. This can be a self-assessment by the provider or involve a third-party Notified Body.

**Data Governance**
: The overall framework for managing data throughout its lifecycle (collection, storage, use, protection, deletion) to ensure compliance with regulations (like GDPR and the AI Act's Article 10) and organizational policies. This includes aspects like data quality, bias mitigation, and relevance for AI systems.

**General Purpose AI Model (GPAIM)**
: An AI model, including when trained with a large amount of data using self-supervision at scale, that displays significant generality and is capable of competently performing a wide range of distinct tasks regardless of the way the model is placed on the market and that can be integrated into a variety of downstream systems or applications.

**GPAIM with Systemic Risk**
: A General Purpose AI Model that has high-impact capabilities, evaluated based on criteria like computational power used for training (e.g., cumulative compute threshold like 10^25 FLOPs) or other designations by the Commission. These models are subject to additional, stricter obligations under the AI Act.

**High-Risk AI System**
: An AI system identified as high-risk based on its intended purpose, either because it falls into one of the categories listed in Annex III (e.g., employment, critical infrastructure, law enforcement) or because it is a safety component of a product covered by specific EU harmonization legislation listed in Annex II. These systems face mandatory compliance requirements.

**Human Oversight**
: The measures and procedures ensuring that humans can effectively oversee the functioning of an AI system. This includes the ability to fully understand the system's capabilities and limitations, remain aware of potential automation bias, correctly interpret the system's output, decide when _not_ to use the system or to disregard its output, and intervene in its operation or deactivate it when necessary.

**Limited Risk AI System**
: AI systems that require specific transparency obligations, such as informing users they are interacting with an AI (chatbots) or that content is AI-generated (deepfakes).

**Market Surveillance Authority**
: The national authority in an EU Member State responsible for monitoring AI systems placed on the market, ensuring they comply with the AI Act, investigating non-compliance, and enforcing the regulation.

**Minimal or No Risk AI System**
: AI systems that do not fall into the unacceptable, high, or limited risk categories. These systems face no specific obligations under the AI Act itself but must comply with other applicable laws (e.g., GDPR).

**Notified Body**
: An independent organization designated by an EU Member State to perform third-party conformity assessments for certain high-risk AI systems before they can be placed on the market with a CE marking.

**Prohibited AI Practices**
: Specific applications of AI that are banned under the AI Act (Article 5) because they pose an unacceptable risk to fundamental rights (e.g., social scoring by governments, certain manipulative AI, most real-time remote biometric identification by law enforcement in public spaces).

**Provider**
: A natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body that develops an AI system or that has an AI system developed and places that system on the market or puts it into service under its own name or trademark, whether for payment or free of charge.

**User (Deployer)**
: Any natural or legal person, public authority, agency or other body using an AI system under its authority, except where the AI system is used in the course of a personal non-professional activity.

**Algorithmic Bias**
: Systematic and repeatable errors in a computer system that create unfair outcomes, such as privileging one arbitrary group of users over others. In the context of AI, this often arises from biased training data or flawed model design.

**Deepfake**
: AI-generated or manipulated image, audio, or video content that depicts a person appearing to say or do something they did not actually say or do, or presents events that did not actually occur. Subject to transparency obligations under the AI Act.

**Explainability**
: The degree to which the internal mechanics of an AI system can be explained in human terms. High-risk AI systems must be designed to be sufficiently transparent for users to interpret and appropriately use their outputs.

**Foundation Model**
: A large AI model trained on broad data at scale, designed to be adapted to a wide range of downstream tasks. Often synonymous with or a subset of General Purpose AI Models (GPAIMs).

**Post-Market Monitoring**
: The systematic process of collecting and analyzing data on AI system performance after market placement to identify and address issues, ensure continued compliance, and inform corrective actions.

**Real-Time Remote Biometric Identification**
: The use of an AI system to identify natural persons at a distance through comparison of biometric data with reference data in databases, where capture, comparison, and identification occur without significant delay. Generally prohibited in public spaces for law enforcement under the AI Act.

**Sandbox (AI Regulatory Sandbox)**
: A controlled framework set up by competent authorities that offers innovators the opportunity to develop and test innovative AI systems under regulatory supervision, often with temporary exemptions from certain requirements.

**Systemic Risk**
: Risks associated with GPAIMs that could have a significant impact on the Union market due to their reach, or actual or reasonably foreseeable negative effects on public health, safety, security, fundamental rights, or society as a whole.

---

_Definitions are based on the provided document and interpretations of the EU AI Act. Refer to the official legal text for precise definitions._
