# Category: Limited Risk AI Systems

## 1. Definition

Limited Risk AI systems are those that do not pose significant threats to health, safety, or fundamental rights like high-risk systems, but still warrant specific **transparency obligations**. The primary goal for this category is to ensure that individuals are aware when they are interacting with an AI system or encountering AI-generated content, allowing them to make informed decisions.

Compliance for this category focuses almost exclusively on disclosure and transparency.

## 2. Examples of Limited Risk AI Systems & Obligations

The AI Act specifies certain types of AI systems that fall under this category and outlines their respective transparency duties:

- **AI Systems Intended to Interact Directly with Natural Persons (e.g., Chatbots):**

  - **Obligation:** Users must be informed that they are interacting with an AI system, unless this is obvious from the context or circumstances of use.
  - _Example:_ A customer service chatbot must clearly state it's an AI at the beginning of the conversation.

- **Emotion Recognition Systems & Biometric Categorization Systems:**

  - **Obligation:** Individuals exposed to these systems must be informed of their operation. (Note: Use in workplace/education is often prohibited; use by law enforcement may be high-risk).
  - _Example:_ If a system analyzes customer facial expressions in a store for marketing insights (where permitted), customers should be notified.

- **AI Systems Generating or Manipulating Content ('Deepfakes'):**

  - **Obligation:** AI systems generating or manipulating image, audio, or video content that appreciably resembles existing persons, objects, places, or entities or events, and would falsely appear authentic or truthful ('deepfake'), must disclose that the content has been artificially generated or manipulated.
  - **Obligation:** Similarly, AI systems generating or manipulating text published to inform the public on matters of public interest must disclose the artificial generation, unless reviewed by humans for editorial control.
  - **Exception:** Disclosure is not required if necessary for exercising freedom of expression, arts, sciences, or for authorized law enforcement purposes (subject to safeguards).
  - _Example:_ An application creating realistic synthetic profile pictures must label them as AI-generated. A news article generated by AI must be labeled as such if not human-edited.

- **General Purpose AI Models (GPAIMs) - Specific Transparency:**
  - **Obligation:** Developers of generative AI models must ensure outputs are marked in a machine-readable format and detectable as artificially generated. ([See Main README Section 4](../README.md))

## 3. Key Obligations

The core requirement for this category is **Transparency**:

- **Disclosure of AI Interaction:** Making it clear when a human is interacting with an AI.
- **Disclosure of AI-Generated Content:** Labeling or marking content (images, video, audio, text in some cases) created or significantly altered by AI.
- **Disclosure of Emotion Recognition/Biometric Categorization:** Informing individuals when such systems are in operation.

## 4. Associated Technologies & Risks

Technologies commonly found in limited-risk systems include:

- **Natural Language Processing (NLP) / Natural Language Generation (NLG):** Powering chatbots and text generation.
  - _Risks:_ Deception if users believe they are interacting with a human, potential for manipulation, generation of misinformation if not labeled.
- **Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), Diffusion Models, Transformers:** Used for creating deepfakes, synthetic media, and AI art.
  - _Risks:_ Spread of disinformation, reputational damage, fraud, erosion of trust in media if not properly disclosed.
- **Computer Vision & Affective Computing:** Used in emotion recognition and biometric categorization.
  - _Risks:_ Inaccuracy, potential for bias, privacy concerns, chilling effects if users are unaware of monitoring.

## 5. Implications for Projects

If your AI project falls into the limited risk category, the primary focus is on implementing clear and effective transparency mechanisms:

1.  **Identify Interaction Points:** Determine where users interact with the AI or AI-generated content.
2.  **Implement Disclosure:** Design and integrate clear notifications, labels, watermarks, or metadata as required by the specific use case (chatbot identification, deepfake labeling, etc.).
3.  **Consider User Experience:** Ensure disclosures are understandable and accessible without being overly intrusive.
4.  **Document Compliance:** Keep records of how transparency obligations are met.

While less burdensome than high-risk compliance, failing to meet transparency requirements can still result in penalties.
